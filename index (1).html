The One God: Allah, Yahweh, Elohim

Allah (الله)

Etymology: Arabic contraction of "Al-Ilah" meaning "The God"

Semitic Root: ʾ-l-h (to worship, divinity)

Biblical References: Aramaic "Elah" used in Daniel 2:47

Historical Usage: Pre-Islamic Arab Christians and Jews used "Allah"

Yahweh (יהוה)

Etymology: Hebrew verb "hayah" (to be, to exist)

Meaning: "He Who Causes to Be" or "The Self-Existent One"

Tetragrammaton: YHWH - The Unpronounceable Name

Quranic Connection: Surah Taha 20:14 "Indeed, I am Allah. There is no deity except Me"

Elohim (אֱלֹהִים)

Etymology: Plural form of "Eloah" from Semitic root "ʾ-l-h"

Usage: Used over 2,500 times in Hebrew Bible

Grammatical Plural: Majesty plural (pluralis majestatis)

Islamic Perspective: Aligns with Allah's 99 Names of Majesty
