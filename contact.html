Frequently Asked Questions

Is this website only for Muslims?

No, our content is designed for everyone - Muslims seeking to deepen their knowledge, non-Muslims exploring Islam, students of comparative religion, and anyone interested in evidence-based understanding of Islam. We provide resources suitable for all backgrounds and levels of understanding.

How authentic are your sources?

We use only authentic Islamic sources (Quran with verified translations, Sahih Hadith collections), academic biblical studies, peer-reviewed scientific research, and historical documents. All references are properly cited and cross-checked by our team of scholars to ensure accuracy and authenticity.

Do you offer one-on-one counseling?

Yes, we have qualified scholars available for personal guidance on Islamic matters, comparative religion questions, and spiritual counseling. You can schedule a consultation through our contact form by selecting "Islamic Questions" as your subject.

Can I contribute content to your website?

Yes, we welcome scholarly contributions from qualified individuals. Please contact us with your qualifications and proposed topics for review. All submissions undergo a rigorous review process by our editorial board to ensure they meet our standards of accuracy and quality.

Are there physical classes or is everything online?

Currently all classes are conducted online through our learning platform, making them accessible to students worldwide. We plan to establish physical centers in the future insha'Allah. However, our online platform provides interactive live sessions, recorded lectures, and one-on-one mentorship.

Are the courses free or paid?

All basic Islamic education courses are completely free as part of our mission to spread authentic Islamic knowledge. We also offer advanced certificate programs with nominal fees to support our operations. Scholarships are available for those who cannot afford the fees.
